Agricultural Vocabulary

  • Agriculture – The profession and process of making food available to the populace through planting of crops and rearing of animals for evenual consumption of human beings.
  • Plants & Crops – They can be used interchangeably although all crops are plants but all plants are not crops. plants grow naturally with such examples as grass, flowers, trees and so on but crops have to be intentionally cultivated or planted.
  • Mixed farming – The act of planting crops and rearing of animals on the same expense of land so that animals can consume vegetation while plants can make use of animal dungs/waste in terms of soil enrichment for plants to grow properly.
  • Subsistence farming – A type of crop production in which the farmer is only intrested in producing food for his family only.
  • Comercial Farming – Farming for the purpose of selling a lot of the harvest.
  • Crop Rotation – In this system, the farmer plants a particular crop for 2-3 years and then changes to other crops later shifting cultivation.
  • Farm House – A make shift building of mud and thatched roof in which the farmers rests and keeps his/her tools/implements.
  • Grafting – A science based process of inserting the stem or root of a different fruit into the side of an original tree stem, waiting for the inserted stem or root or leave to grow and produce its own fruit.
  • Plantation – Large expanse of land, usually in hecters, used to cultivate major crops such as cocoa, cotton, plantain, sugar, rice, palm tree and so on.
  • Humus – The dark top soil in which people plant.
  • Manure – Animal waste/dung which farmers use to enrich the soil.
  • Compost – Mixture of animal dung, waste products, urine, leaves, crops all left to rot and used to enrich the soil.
  • Seeds – Usually small organic parts of crops used for reproduction of such crops eg(orange seeds, tangerine seeds, Mango seeds, and so on).
  • Seedlings – Small green delicate plants uprooted for replanting on ridges.
  • Mechanised Farming – This is the application of technology in Agricultural in which machines are used to produce crops in mass.
