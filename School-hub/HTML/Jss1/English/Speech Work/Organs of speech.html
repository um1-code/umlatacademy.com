English JSS1 – Speech Work

Topic: Organs of speech

Learning Objectives

    By the end of this lesson, students should be able to:

  • Identify the major organs of speech.
  • Explain how these organs help in producing speech sounds.
  • Describe the role of each organ with examples.

Introduction

Speech is made possible by different parts of the body working together. These parts are called the organs of speech. They help us create and control sounds when we speak. The organs involved in speech are found in the mouth, nose, throat, and lungs.

Definition

Organs of speech are the parts of the human body used to produce sounds. These include the respiratory organs and the vocal apparatus.

The Major Organs of Speech are
Organ Function
Lungs Push out air that is used to produce sounds.
Trachea (Windpipe) Carries air from the lungs to the larynx, where sound production begins.
Larynx (Voice Box) Contains the vocal cords (or vocal folds), which vibrate to produce sound.
Pharynx (Throat) Acts as a passage for air to reach the mouth or nose.
Nasal Cavity Produces nasal sounds like /m/, /n/, /ŋ/ (as in "sing").
Oral Cavity Where most speech sounds are shaped (tongue, teeth, lips).
Teeth Help to pronounce certain sounds like /t/, /d/, /s/.
Tongue Helps to form various sounds by moving to different parts of the mouth.
Lips Help in producing sounds like /p/, /b/, /m/.
Alveolar Ridge The ridge behind the upper front teeth; helps in producing sounds like /t/, /d/.
Soft Palate (Velum) Moves to block the nasal passage to allow for oral sounds.

Examples of Sounds Produced
Sound Organ(s) Involved
/m/, /n/ Nasal cavity and mouth
/p/, /b/ Lips
/t/, /d/ Tongue and alveolar ridge
/k/, /g/ Tongue and soft palate
/s/, /z/ Teeth and tongue

Summary

    In this lesson, we have learned:

  • Speech organs are parts of the body used to produce sounds.
  • These organs work together in different ways to create different sounds used in speech.
  • Understanding these organs helps improve pronunciation and speech clarity.

Practice Questions

  1. What are the speech organs and why are they important?
  2. List five organs of speech and explain their functions.
  3. How does the tongue help in speech production?
