English JSS1 – Speech Work

Topic: Speech Sounds

Learning Objectives

  • Identify the different types of speech sounds
  • Distinguish between vowels and consonants
  • Pronounce simple English words correctly

Introduction

Speech work is the part of English that focuses on how words are pronounced. It involves understanding sounds, stress, and intonation in spoken English.

1. What are Speech Sounds?

Speech sounds are the sounds we make when speaking. In English, there are two main types:

  • Vowels: Sounds made without blocking the air. Examples: /a/, /e/, /i/, /o/, /u/
  • Consonants: Sounds made when the air is partly or completely blocked. Examples: /b/, /d/, /s/, /k/

2. Examples of Vowel Sounds:

  • /i:/ – as in “see”
  • /ɪ/ – as in “sit”
  • /æ/ – as in “cat”

3. Examples of Consonant Sounds:

  • /p/ – as in “pen”
  • /t/ – as in “top”
  • /m/ – as in “man”

4. Importance of Correct Pronunciation:

Good pronunciation helps others understand you clearly. It is important to listen and practice how native or fluent English speakers talk.

Summary

Speech sounds are divided into vowels and consonants. Practicing these sounds helps improve spoken English and communication skills.

Practice Questions

  1. What are the two types of speech sounds?
  2. Give three examples of vowel sounds.
  3. Why is correct pronunciation important?
