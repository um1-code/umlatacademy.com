English JSS1 – Grammar

Topic: Parts of Speech

Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students should be able to:

  • Identify and understand the different parts of speech in English.
  • Understand and correctly use the various tenses in English.
  • Form simple, compound, and complex sentences.
  • Apply correct punctuation in their writing.
  • Avoid common grammar mistakes.

Introduction

Grammar is the foundation of any language. It provides the rules for sentence construction, word usage, and punctuation. Understanding grammar helps us communicate more effectively and express our ideas clearly. In this lesson, we will focus on the basic components of English grammar, including parts of speech, tenses, sentence structure, and punctuation.

1. Parts of Speech

Parts of speech refer to categories that words belong to based on their role in a sentence. The main parts of speech include:

The 8 Parts of Speech:

  • Noun: – Words that name people, places, things, or ideas.

    • Examples: dog, city, happiness, school

  • Pronoun – Words that take the place of nouns.

    • Examples: he, she, it, they

  • Verb – Words that describe actions or states of being.

    • Examples: run, eat, is, were

  • Adjective – Words that describe nouns or pronouns.

    • Examples: tall, blue, happy

  • Adverb – Words that describe verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs.

    • Examples: quickly, very, slowly

  • Preposition – Words that show the relationship between a noun (or pronoun) and another word in the sentence.

    • Examples: in, on, at, under

  • Conjunction – Words that join words, phrases, or clauses.

    • Examples: and, but, or, because

  • Interjection – Words that express strong feelings or emotions.

    • Examples: oh, wow, ouch
