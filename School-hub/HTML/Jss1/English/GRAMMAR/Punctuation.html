4. Punctuation

Punctuation marks are used to clarify meaning in a sentence. Here are the key punctuation marks:

  • Period (.): Ends a statement.

    • Example: I like reading books.

  • Comma (,): Separates items in a list, clauses, or phrases.

    • Example: I bought apples, oranges, and bananas.

  • Question Mark (?):Ends a question.

    • Example: How are you?

  • Exclamation Mark (!): Shows strong feelings or a command.

    • Example: Wow, that was amazing!

  • Apostrophe ('): Shows possession or creates contractions.

    • Example: Sarah’s book, I can’t believe it.

  • Quotation Marks (" "): Used for direct speech or quotations.

    • Example: He said, "I will be there soon."

Summary

In this lesson, we have learned the basic rules of English grammar. We studied the parts of speech, the different tenses, the sentence structures, and the important punctuation marks that help us communicate clearly. Mastering these grammar rules will help you write and speak more effectively.
