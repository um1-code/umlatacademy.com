2. Tenses

Tenses tell us when an action or event happens. There are three main tenses: present, past, and future. Each tense has different forms.

The 3 types of tenses:

  • Present Tense
  • Past Tense
  • Future Tense

-Present Tense

The 4 types of Present tense:

  • Simple Present:Describes habitual actions or facts.

    • Example: I play football every day.

  • Present Continuous: Describes actions happening right now.

    • Example: She is reading a book.

  • Present Perfect: Describes actions that happened at an unspecified time in the past.

    • Example: They have finished their homework.

  • Present Perfect Continuous:Describes actions that started in the past and continue to the present.

    • Example: I have been studying for two hours.

-Past Tense

The 4 types of Past Tense:

  • Simple Past: Describes actions that were completed in the past.

    • Example: He visited his grandmother yesterday.

  • Past Continuous:Describes actions that were happening at a specific time in the past.

    • Example: I was reading when the phone rang.

  • Past Perfect:Describes actions completed before another action in the past.

    • Example: She had already left when I arrived.

  • Past Perfect Continuous: Describes actions that were ongoing in the past before another action.

    • Example: They had been waiting for an hour when I arrived.

-Future Tense

The 4 types of -Future Tense:

  • Simple Future:Describes actions that will happen in the future.

    • Example: I will travel to Lagos next week.

  • Future Continuous:Describes actions that will be ongoing at a specific time in the future.

    • Example: He will be studying at 8 PM.

  • Future Perfect:Describes actions that will be completed by a certain future time.

    • Example: We will have finished the project by next week.

  • Future Perfect Continuous:Describes actions that will have been ongoing by a certain future time.

    • Example: I will have been working for five hours by noon.
